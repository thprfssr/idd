The Danetian Language

Danetian is a constructed language belonging to the Indo-European family. It was created by Javier Castro beginning in the summer of 2017, originally intended to be his own personal classical language. However, the project has evolved over the years, so that it now feels like a typical modern European language.

The majority of the lexicon is directly inherited from Proto-Indo-European in the natural development of the language. However, there is also a large number of borrowings from Latin and from Proto-Germanic, and a bit from the Turkic languages.

As is expected from an Indo-European language, nouns in Danetian inflect according to case. In fact, the language has preserved seven of the eight cases from Proto-Indo-European. Moreover, the language has developed an extensive yet simple system of verbal inflection. Therefore, Danetian undoubtedly finds itself in the company of other synthetic languages.

In this website, you will find everything there is to know about Danetian, from its lively inflectional morphology to its unique syntactic constructions.
